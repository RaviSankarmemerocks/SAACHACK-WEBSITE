TERMS AND CONDITIONS

Eligibility for student team members:

1. Technical students from AICTE/ UGC/ IIT/ NIT/other govt. approved institutions.
2. The team can consist of 4 members at max(not less than 2).
3. All the team members should be studying in the same institution.
4. Team members may be from different academic levels.
5. Mentor should present along with the team members.
6. Teams should be made up exclusively of students with ID cards.
7. The admission of teams to the competition is on a first-come, first-served basis and is to be limited to not more than 50 teams
8. All team members should be present at the event. Leaving the venue for some time to hack elsewhere is fine.
9. The partial problems will be given one month prior to the event date and the teams can choose any one from the given track. The Teams should submit the partial solution documents within fifteen days. From this top 50 teams will be scrutinized. Once a problem is chosen it cannot be changed.
10. At the time of Hackathon the remaining problem will be given. And the Hackathon teams should complete the task within 24 hours. All the remaining work on a project should be done at the time of the Hackathon.
11. Teams can use an idea they had before the event.
12. Teams can use libraries, frameworks, or open-source code in their projects. Working on a project before the event and open-sourcing it for the sole purpose of using the code during the event is against the spirit of the rules and is not allowed.
13. Adding new features to existing projects is allowed. Judges will only consider new functionality introduced or new features added during the Hackathon in determining the winners.
14. Teams must stop hacking once the time is up. However, teams are allowed to debug and make small fixes to their programs after time is up. Making large changes or adding new features is not allowed.
15. Projects that violate the Code of Conduct are not allowed.
16. Teams can be disqualified from the competition at the organizers' discretion. Reasons might include but are not limited to breaking the Competition Rules, breaking the Code of Conduct, or other unsporting behavior.
